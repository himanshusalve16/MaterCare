Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about maternal health.

When should I start prenatal care?

It's recommended to start prenatal care as soon as you know you're pregnant, ideally within the first 8 weeks of pregnancy. Early prenatal care is crucial for monitoring your health and your baby's development.

What vitamins should I take during pregnancy?

Essential prenatal vitamins include folic acid, iron, calcium, and vitamin D. Consult with your healthcare provider for personalized recommendations based on your specific needs.

Is it safe to exercise during pregnancy?

Yes, moderate exercise is generally safe and beneficial during pregnancy. However, always consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise routine and follow safe pregnancy exercise guidelines.

What foods should I avoid during pregnancy?

Avoid raw or undercooked meat, unpasteurized dairy products, certain types of fish high in mercury, and unwashed fruits and vegetables. Consult our nutrition guide for a complete list of foods to avoid.
